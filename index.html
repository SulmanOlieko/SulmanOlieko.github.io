Bio

Sulman is an agricultural and environmental economist committed to leveraging research to shape policies that drive the sustainable transformation of the agri-food sector.

Sulman is an award-winning student. In August, 2023, he emerged the best Collaborative Masters in Agricultural and Applied Economics (CMAAE) student in the SFSE, a program that brings together 8 major universities across East and Southern Africa. Earlier on in May, the same year, he had obtained recognition for emerging the second-best masters student in the Faculty of Agriculture, University of Nairobi, in academic performance.

His key expertise lie in the areas of Production economics, Econometrics, Statistics, and Quantitative modeling of agricultural and environmental policy evaluation techniques. He applies diverse skills from these fields in analyzing food security, agricultural and environmental productivity, efficiency, sustainability, gender differentials, climate change effects, contingent valuation and choice experiments, and impact evaluation, to derive actionable insights that can inform sound policies necessary for the agri-food sector transformation.

Currently, Sulman is a Research Fellow at the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) under the Agroecology Initiative of the OneCGIAR. He is also a seasoned teaching assistant at the Department of Agricultural Economics, University of Nairobi.
